Instructions

Using Bluetooth

Please read this thoroughly before proceed to execute the pairing instruction!!!

1 - Exit the app and go to your settings to enable Bluetooth.
2 - Take out the battery, if not already removed, from your blood pressure monitor. Insert them back in. Turning on the device is optional. 2 - Search for devices and connect to your Bluetooth enabled blood pressure monitor. Its name should start with "A&D BP".
3 - Wait until it becomes a paired device.
4 - Your A&D Blood Pressure Monitor will now be able to use its bluetooth enabled measuring method.

Using Home Blood Pressure Monitoring (HBPM)

1 - Select Mode Selection on the main menu and then select HBPM Mode.
2 - Setup the start date, end date and the times you wish to receive reminders to take a measurement.
3 - Select “Use Bluetooth” if you wish to take a measurement via Bluetooth (See ‘Using Bluetooth’ section)
4 - Click next and take measurements. You must be seated.
5 - For each blood pressure recording, two consecutive measurements are taken, at least 1 minute apart
6 - Blood pressure should be recorded twice daily, ideally in the morning and evening 7 - Blood pressure recording continues for at least 4 days, ideally for 7 days.
8 - To edit the settings of your current HBPM course select Current Activity in the main menu, then select Edit Setup.
9 - To take another measurement in your current HBPM course select Current Activity in the main menu then select Take a Measurement.

Using Free Mode

1 - Select Mode Selection on the main menu and then select Free Mode
2 - Select “Turn on Bluetooth” if you wish to take a measurement via Bluetooth (See ‘Using Bluetooth’ section)
3 - Enter your blood pressure measurements

Records

1 - Select records in the Main Menu
2 - Send Records – select the records you wish to send to your GP and press send
3 - Delete Records – select the records you wish to delete and press Delete

Edit Personal Details

1 - Select Personal Info in the main menu then select Edit.

Disclaimer
The best use for this app is for GPs, who are either fully self employed or have a mixed income. Though there is scope for other specialty doctors and self employed people to use this app (as the field can be tailored to the user) - the recommendations are for the former group.

This app is designed to be used to assist the tracking of income and expenditure as well as provided enhanced features such as reminders, photo evidence and help with pension calculations for NHS pensionable incomes earned. The user would still need to do their locum forms if they are paying into a NHS pension.

By using the app, the user still has to take full responsibility of their accounts. They would be expected to monitor their own data and declare them to the appropriate bodies in their country of use (eg HMRC in UK).

It is recommended the user reviews their income and expenditure on regular intervals.

The spreadsheet is designed to be utilised straight away and carries all the data that the user inputs. The data on the spreadsheet will be a reflection of how the user manages their entries in the app.

The app makers cannot replace an accountant’s job and it is recommended the user utilises an accountant as per normal.

It is also up to the user to keep records of their documentation as per HMRC guidance.
